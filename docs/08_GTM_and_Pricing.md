# 08 — Go-To-Market & Pricing Strategy

## 1. Target customer (ICP)
**Primary (Phases 1–3):** mid-market residential developers, 1–5 active projects, 100–500 units/project, ₹40L–₹2Cr ticket, in RJ/MH/NCR/GJ Tier-1&2 — big enough to feel the collections pain, too small for Salesforce/enterprise ERPs. **Secondary (Phase 4):** strata-commercial developers and single-asset mall/high-street owners in Tier-2. **Anti-ICP (for now):** top-10 national developers (long procurement, custom demands), pure rental brokers, RWAs.

## 2. Packaging (feature-flag driven)
| Package | Contents | Indicative price |
|---|---|---|
| **Launch** | Tower Explorer (Tier-1), cost sheets, EOI booking, CP portal, branded site/PWA | ₹2.5–4L/yr per project + one-time setup ₹1–2L |
| **Lifecycle** | + CLP autopilot, demand letters, Digital Khata, payments, progress feed, RERA export | ₹6–10L/yr per project (or ₹1,500–2,500/unit one-time equivalent) |
| **Possession+** | + snagging/handover, loan desk, referrals, NRI mode, analytics | +₹2–3L/yr |
| **Commercial/Mall** | Stacking plan, leasing, CAM, tenant portal, investor payouts | ₹5–15L/yr per asset by GLA |
| Add-ons | Full 3D twin (per-project content), drone service, extra languages, custom domain apps | à la carte |

Rationale: per-project pricing matches how builders budget (project P&L, RERA account can pay for it); payments flowing through the platform later enable a transaction-fee line (keep MDR pass-through transparent).

## 3. Sales motion
1. **Design-partner case study first.** Phase-1 partner gets 70–80% discount for data rights + testimonial + site-office demo access. The pitch deck is their live app.
2. **Demo weapon:** a fictional "Skyline Heights" full-stack demo (like Riyasat for plots) — sales can hand any builder their *own logo* on it within a day (theming tokens make this cheap). "Your project, live in 7 days."
3. **Wedge pitch = money, not software:** open with the collections math — "manual demand letters delay ₹X crores by Y weeks; we cut it to 24 hours" — then show the buyer delight as the differentiator.
4. **Channels:** CREDAI chapter events (Jaipur/Rajasthan first), RERA-consultant and CA partnerships (they advise builders on compliance — our RERA-audit-ready trail is their referral reason), channel-partner networks (CPs who love the portal pull builders), LinkedIn founder-led content with progress-feed screenshots.
5. **Land-and-expand:** land with Launch on one project → expand to Lifecycle at first slab (when collections pain peaks) → all projects → commercial assets.

## 4. Onboarding playbook (make it a product)
Day 1–2: theming + domain + project CMS. Day 3–4: floor plates traced, units imported via guided Excel importer, rate cards. Day 5: payment gateway + WhatsApp templates (pre-warmed). Day 6: CP onboarding webinar (Hindi). Day 7: launch event with live EOI. Include a **content kit**: drone shoot scheduling, photo guidelines for site engineers, template pack (allotment/agreement/demand letters vetted per state).

## 5. Success metrics (business)
ARR per tenant, logo count, project count, % tenants on Lifecycle+, GMV of payments processed, net revenue retention (target > 120% via expansion), CAC payback < 9 months, design-partner NPS.

## 6. Competitive responses to expect
- CRMs (Sell.Do/DaeBuild) improving buyer apps → our moat: consumer-grade UX + visual inventory + evidence-gating loop; keep shipping buyer-side.
- 3D vendors adding booking → they lack ledger/CRM depth; partner with one for the premium tier before they compete.
- Price war at the low end → hold the money-features value line; Launch tier can flex.

## 7. 90-day GTM checklist
☐ Sign residential design partner (LOI, discount terms, data rights). ☐ "Skyline Heights" demo tenant built. ☐ Collections-math ROI calculator (one-pager + spreadsheet) for sales. ☐ CREDAI Jaipur event presence booked. ☐ 3 CA/RERA-consultant referral partners. ☐ Pricing sheet + MSA + DPA (DPDP-compliant) templates legal-reviewed. ☐ Case-study capture plan (before/after collection days, booking conversion).
